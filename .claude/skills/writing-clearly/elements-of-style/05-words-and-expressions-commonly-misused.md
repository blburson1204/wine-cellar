# V. Words and Expressions Commonly Misused

Word choice guidance. If the writer makes it his purpose to express accurately
his own thought and refuses to be satisfied with a ready-made formula, most of
these will cause little trouble.

---

## Quick Reference Table

| Word/Expression     | Guidance                                                                                             |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **All right**       | Always two words.                                                                                    |
| **As to whether**   | _Whether_ is sufficient.                                                                             |
| **Can**             | Means _am/is/are able_. Not a substitute for _may_.                                                  |
| **Certainly**       | Used indiscriminately to intensify. A mannerism, bad in speech, worse in writing.                    |
| **Data**            | Plural, like _phenomena_ and _strata_.                                                               |
| **Different than**  | Not permissible. Use _different from_, _other than_, or _unlike_.                                    |
| **Etc.**            | Not to be used if the reader would be left in doubt. Incorrect after _such as_ or _for example_.     |
| **Fact**            | Only for matters capable of direct verification, not matters of judgment.                            |
| **However**         | In the meaning _nevertheless_, not to come first in its sentence or clause.                          |
| **Interesting**     | Avoid as perfunctory introduction. Instead of announcing interest, create it.                        |
| **Less**            | Do not misuse for _fewer_. _Less_ refers to quantity, _fewer_ to number.                             |
| **Like**            | Not to be misused for _as_. _Like_ governs nouns and pronouns; before phrases and clauses, use _as_. |
| **Most**            | Not to be used for _almost_.                                                                         |
| **Nature**          | Often simply redundant. "Acts of a hostile nature" = "Hostile acts."                                 |
| **One of the most** | Avoid beginning essays or paragraphs with this threadbare formula.                                   |
| **Possess**         | Not a mere substitute for _have_ or _own_.                                                           |
| **So**              | Avoid as an intensifier in writing: "so good," "so warm."                                            |
| **State**           | Not a mere substitute for _say_ or _remark_. Restrict to _express fully or clearly_.                 |
| **Very**            | Use sparingly. Where emphasis is necessary, use words strong in themselves.                          |
| **While**           | Best restricted to _during the time that_. Avoid for _and_, _but_, _although_.                       |

## Wordy Expressions to Cut

| Wordy                             | Concise              |
| --------------------------------- | -------------------- |
| In many cases                     | Often                |
| Acts of a hostile character       | Hostile acts         |
| He is a man who is very ambitious | He is very ambitious |
| owing to the fact that            | since (because)      |
| in spite of the fact that         | though (although)    |
| the fact that I had arrived       | my arrival           |

---

## Compare / Consider

**Compare.** _Compare to_ points out resemblances between things of different
order; _compare with_ points out differences between things of the same order.

**Consider.** Not followed by _as_ when it means "believe to be." "I consider
him competent" — not "I consider him as competent."

---

## Due to

Incorrectly used for _through_, _because of_, or _owing to_ in adverbial
phrases. Correct only as predicate or modifier of a particular noun:

| Incorrect                                    | Correct                                         |
| -------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| He lost the first game, due to carelessness. | He lost the first game because of carelessness. |
