# Signs of AI Writing

Research-based guide to detecting and eliminating AI-generated writing patterns.
Based on Wikipedia editor research into LLM output characteristics. These
patterns emerge because LLMs regress to statistical means — specific details
blur into generic statements, and common training-data phrases dominate output.

Use this reference to audit and clean your own output.

---

## Regression to the Mean

AI tends to replace specific facts with broad generalizations. The more specific
the original, the more likely the AI replaces it with something vague and
inflated.

| AI Pattern                        | What Humans Write                             |
| --------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| "revolutionary titan of industry" | "inventor of the first train-coupling device" |
| "a beacon of progress"            | name the specific contribution                |
| "played a key role in shaping"    | describe the actual action taken              |

---

## Puffery and Undue Emphasis

Overused phrases that inflate importance without adding information:

- "stands as"
- "testament to"
- "plays a vital role"
- "symbolizing lasting impact"
- "key turning point"
- "enduring legacy"
- "cornerstone of"
- "pivotal moment"
- "remains a towering figure"

**Fix:** State the actual consequence or contribution. Let the facts carry the
weight.

---

## Promotional Language

Words and phrases that read like tourism brochures or press releases:

- "nestled" (in the heart of, among the hills)
- "breathtaking"
- "vibrant"
- "continues to captivate"
- "stunning natural beauty"
- "boasts a" (rich history, diverse population)
- "world-renowned"
- "unparalleled"
- "trailblazing"

**Fix:** Describe what the thing actually is or does. Drop the adjective; add a
fact.

---

## Superficial Analysis via Present Participles

AI uses present participles to make inanimate subjects perform actions they
cannot perform. Facts do not "highlight" or "underscore" — people do.

| AI Pattern                          | Direct Version            |
| ----------------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| "highlighting the importance of"    | state why it matters      |
| "underscoring the significance"     | state the significance    |
| "showcasing the capabilities"       | describe the capabilities |
| "fostering a sense of community"    | describe what happened    |
| "ensuring long-term sustainability" | describe the mechanism    |
| "driving innovation forward"        | name the innovation       |

## Overused AI Vocabulary

Words that appear disproportionately in LLM output compared to human writing:

### High-frequency offenders

align with, multifaceted, intricate, tapestry, landscape, robust, seamless,
leverage, underscore, utilize, facilitate, optimize, harness, empower, bolster,
spearhead, streamline, foster, delve, nuanced, holistic

### Filler intensifiers

crucial, pivotal, vital, paramount, indispensable, transformative,
groundbreaking, cutting-edge, state-of-the-art

### Vague connectors

in terms of, with regard to, in the context of, it is worth noting that, it
should be noted that

**Fix:** Replace each with the specific word the sentence needs. "Leverage"
becomes "use." "Utilize" becomes "use." "Facilitate" becomes whatever the actual
action is. "Robust" becomes a description of what makes the thing strong.

---

## Structural Tells

### Conclusions that restate the introduction

AI frequently ends documents by restating the opening paragraph in slightly
different words. Cut the conclusion or make it say something new.

### "Despite challenges" formula

A rigid pattern: acknowledge difficulty, then pivot to positive outcome. Real
writing addresses the difficulty specifically or omits the formula entirely.

### Rule of three

AI triples adjectives and phrases compulsively: "innovative, dynamic, and
forward-thinking." If all three words say the same thing, keep one.

### Excessive em dashes

Formulaic use of em dashes to create a punched-up, sales-copy rhythm. Use em
dashes sparingly and only when the interruption serves meaning.

### Excessive formatting

- Unnecessary bullet lists where prose works better
- Bold on every other word
- Emoji decorations in section headings
- "1. **Bold header:** text" formatting for every item

---

## Communication Red Flags

These patterns indicate unedited AI output pasted directly:

- "I hope this helps" / "Let me know if you need anything else"
- "As of my last training update" / "information is limited"
- "Great question!" / "Absolutely!" / "Certainly!"
- Disclaimer language about being an AI
- Knowledge-cutoff hedging

**Fix:** Remove all meta-commentary about the writing process. State the
information directly.

---

## What This Is Not

These are not reliable indicators of AI writing:

- Perfect grammar alone (skilled humans write grammatically)
- Formal tone (some people write formally)
- Sophisticated vocabulary (not inherently artificial)
- Consistent formatting (good writers format consistently)
- Thorough coverage (thoroughness is a virtue, not a tell)

The tells above are about _patterns of evasion_ — replacing specifics with
generalities, inflating importance, and defaulting to formulaic structure. Human
writers can exhibit any individual pattern; it is the combination and density
that signals AI origin.
