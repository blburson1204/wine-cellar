# IV. A Few Matters of Form

Formatting standards for headings, numerals, quotations, references, and
syllabication.

---

## Headings

Leave a blank line after the title or heading of a manuscript.

## Numerals

Do not spell out dates or other serial numbers. Write them in figures or in
Roman numerals:

- August 9, 1918
- Chapter XII
- Rule 3

## Parentheses

A sentence containing an expression in parentheses is punctuated, outside the
marks, exactly as if the expression were absent. The expression within is
punctuated as if it stood by itself, except that the final stop is omitted
unless it is a question mark or exclamation point.

## Quotations

Formal quotations, cited as documentary evidence, are introduced by a colon and
enclosed in quotation marks. Quotations grammatically in apposition or the
direct objects of verbs are preceded by a comma.

Quotations of an entire line or more of verse are begun on a fresh line, set off
by indentation, and need no quotation marks.

## References

In scholarly work requiring exact references, abbreviate titles that occur
frequently. Place references in parentheses or in footnotes, not in the body of
the sentence.

## Titles

Prefer italics with capitalized initials for the titles of literary works.
